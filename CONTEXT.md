Privacy-by-Design dApp Buildathon
Build the Encrypted Fhenix Ecosystem
The window for privacy-native architecture is open. This program is for founders who want to build it in from day one — not retrofit it later.

The Opportunity
Most protocols are architected for transparency by default. That architectural decision defines your user base.

Institutions with compliance requirements, traders protecting strategies, protocols needing sealed-bid mechanics — they can't use transparent rails. Not "won't." Can't.

The cryptographic readiness wasn't there before. Now it is.

Fhenix enables smart contracts to compute on encrypted data using Fully Homomorphic Encryption (FHE). This unlocks applications that are:

Privacy-preserving — data stays encrypted during computation
Programmable — full smart contract logic on encrypted state
Verifiable on-chain — decentralized guarantees without sacrificing confidentiality
Alongside Fhenix, ecosystem tooling like Privara extends what’s possible at the application layer — enabling teams to build confidential payment flows and compliant financial primitives directly into their products.

The protocols that build privacy into their core architecture now will outcompete those trying to retrofit it later.

Why Privacy-by-Design Matters
Public blockchains made transparency the default. That transparency enabled trustless systems — but it also created hard limits on what you can build.

The $500M problem: MEV losses in DeFi. 15–40% of auction value extracted by front-runners. 75% of those losses hitting trades under $20K.

The institutional gap: Major players evaluating on-chain infrastructure won't deploy on transparent rails. Compliance won't allow it.

The retrofit trap: Protocols that ship without privacy will spend years playing catch-up — if they survive the transition at all.

A privacy-by-design approach treats confidentiality as foundational architecture, not an optional feature bolted on later.
